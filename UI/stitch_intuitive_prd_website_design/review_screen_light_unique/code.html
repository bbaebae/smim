Neurobiology Synaptic Plasticity

Describe the molecular mechanism by which NMDA receptors contribute to Long-Term Potentiation (LTP).

During high-frequency stimulation, the postsynaptic membrane depolarizes sufficiently to repel the Mg2+ ion block from the NMDA receptor pore.

When Glutamate binds simultaneously, the channel opens, allowing a significant influx of Ca2+ into the dendritic spine. This calcium acts as a crucial second messenger, activating protein kinases like CaMKII.

These kinases ultimately trigger the exocytosis of additional AMPA receptors into the postsynaptic membrane, leading to a strengthened and sustained synaptic response (LTP).
